Over the last few days, I worked on an e‑commerce analytics dashboard to answer practical business questions, not just plot pretty charts. Using 3 years of sales data, the dashboard brings together revenue, profit, orders, products, and regions to show what’s really driving growth. Here are 5 key questions this dashboard helps answer: 1️⃣ Are we actually growing in a healthy way? The business has generated around $10.67M in total revenue, with 16.3% year‑on‑year growth from 2022 to 2023. At the same time, it maintained about $1.84M in profit with an average margin close to 17%, which means growth is happening without destroying profitability. 2️⃣ How much value do we get from each order? Across 3,500+ orders, the average order value is about $3,048. That’s a strong indicator that customers are buying higher‑value items or multiple products per transaction, which gives room to invest in marketing and customer acquisition. 3️⃣ Which product deserves special focus? One clear hero product stands out: Cameras, contributing around $1.18M in revenue and leading in both sales and profit. This tells the business to highlight Cameras in campaigns, optimize its inventory, and possibly build bundles or upsells around it. 4️⃣ Which region is our biggest opportunity? Region‑wise, the West leads with about $2.84M in revenue. Within that, Electronics are especially strong in the West, making it a prime region for targeted promotions, faster delivery options, and partnerships. Other regions may need tailored strategies to catch up. 5️⃣ Are all categories performing consistently, or are we over‑dependent on a few? The dashboard shows that profit margins are fairly similar across categories (around 17%), which is a good sign. It means the business is not relying on just one category to sustain profits. Combining this with the Top 10 Products and Sales by Category views helps identify where to double down and where to rethink pricing or marketing.
